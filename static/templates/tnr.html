Why Fix?

A big part of Temple Cats’ initiative is reducing the outdoor cat population in Temple University’s campus area.
Many people assume that it is inhumane to fix cats, because it will change their personality, or is inhumane to do. This is false!
Unfixed male cats roam the streets looking for female cats in heat. Crossing big streets increases the chance that they will be hit by cars. When they find a female cat in heat, there will often be other males who fight for mating rights. Female cats are capable of becoming pregnant at just 4 months old, when a pregnancy is difficult because they are too small. Fixing cats is more humane, because they can live longer, safer lives!

Trap - Neuter - Return

TNR is a process where free-roaming outdoor cats are trapped, fixed, and then returned to the outdoors with an ear tip, so they can be identified as fixed.

Benefits

  • Controls cat population
  • Reduces spread of disease by promoting less fighting for territory and mates
  • Lowers cat intake into shelters, therefore reducing euthanasia rates
