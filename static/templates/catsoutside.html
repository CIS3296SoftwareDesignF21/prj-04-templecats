Q: Is it medically urgent or injured?

If the cat is injured or pregnant, and you are able to collect it, you can bring it to ACCT and surrender it, so that it will be medically evaluated. If you are unable to collect it, and it is in Temple Cat’s operating area, you can make a post on Temple Cat’s Facebook group.

Q: Is it friendly?

If the cat is friendly and without an ear tip, and you are able to collect it, you can bring it to a rescue if you call first to ask if they have space. A friendly cat with an ear tip can eventually be brought to a rescue to find a permanent home. A friendly cat without an ear tip could be lost or abandoned. If you are able to, consider temporarily fostering the kitty.

Friendly Cat Assessment

  • Does the cat have a collar, even if it doesn’t have a tag?
  • Does the cat appear ill, with runny eyes or sneezing, heavy breathing, or any unusual behavior?
  • Does the cat look dirty/grungy, as if it has been outside for a while?
  • Can you take pictures of the cat?
  • Can you take note of the location you saw the cat, being as specific as possible?
  • Ask neighbors or people nearby outdoors, if any, if they know if the cat belongs to someone. Sometimes local residents know if a cat is a regular on a street.
